Spotting Leaf Disease Early

You don't need to be a plant pathologist — you just need to know what "wrong" looks like before it spreads.

Look at the pattern, not just the spots

A few isolated brown spots on scattered leaves is often normal wear. Spots that appear suddenly across many trees in the same area, especially after heavy rain, are worth taking seriously.

Check the undersides of leaves, not just the tops

Many fungal issues show up as a powdery or fuzzy coating on the underside first — something a quick glance from a distance will miss entirely.

Watch for unusual leaf drop

Some seasonal leaf fall is completely normal. Sudden, heavy dropping outside the usual season — especially combined with visible spotting — is a stronger warning sign.

The beginner mistake

Waiting to "see if it gets worse" before asking anyone. Disease spreads fastest in the first days it appears. If something looks off, a quick photo sent to a local agricultural officer or an experienced neighbor costs nothing and can save the season.

When in doubt, don't guess — the Rubber Board and local Krishi Bhavan (agriculture office) offer free consultation, and catching a problem early is far cheaper than losing trees to it later.
